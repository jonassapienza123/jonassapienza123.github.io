Biggest surprises in FPL History

On this page, you'll discover the most prolific point-scorers in Fantasy Premier League history — elite players who delivered season after season with unmatched consistency, racking up record-breaking totals and cementing their legacy as FPL greats.

1. Wayne Rooney (2,338 Points)

Wayne Rooney was the embodiment of consistency and versatility during his time in the Premier League. He delivered impressive FPL returns throughout his career thanks to his style of play:

  • Played multiple roles: striker, attacking midfielder, deep-lying playmaker
  • Delivered goals and assists consistently across seasons
  • Excelled in big matches
  • Highly durable and dependable FPL option

2. Frank Lampard (2,318 Points)

Frank Lampard redefined what it meant to be a goal-scoring midfielder. Playing for Chelsea, he regularly notched double-digit goals and assists, making him an FPL manager's dream. His ability to score from open play, penalties, and set pieces made him a premium option nearly every season.

3. Steven Gerrard (2,044 Points)

Steven Gerrard was Liverpool’s talisman and a midfield engine with a constant FPL presence. Renowned for his long-range strikes, pinpoint passing, and leadership, Gerrard contributed heavily in both goals and assists. His set-piece duties further enhanced his fantasy value throughout his career.

4. Mohamed Salah (2,013 Points)

Since arriving at Liverpool in 2017, Mohamed Salah has been a dominant force in FPL. His explosive debut season earned him 303 points, and he has remained one of the top-scoring players ever since. With pace, consistency, and goal involvement, Salah is a modern FPL legend.

5. Petr Čech (1,908 Points)

Petr Čech’s consistency and shot-stopping ability made him the highest-scoring goalkeeper in FPL history. During his time at Chelsea and Arsenal, he regularly brought in clean sheets, save points, and bonuses, making him a favorite set-and-forget option for fantasy managers.
